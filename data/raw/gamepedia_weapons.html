Weapon

From Hearthstone: Heroes of Warcraft Wiki
Jump to: navigation, search

Weapons are special cards which can be equipped by heroes. Each weapon has an Attack value and a Durability value. Each hero can only equip one weapon at a time, and equipping a new weapon will destroy the old one. While a weapon is equipped, the hero's Attack will be increased by an amount equal to the weapon's Attack, and attacking while a weapon is equipped will reduce its Durability by 1. When a weapon's Durability is reduced to zero, the weapon will be destroyed. If a Hero attacks a minion with an Attack value above 0 the minion will deal that amount of damage to the hero in turn.

Weapons can be equipped in several ways. The main source of weapons is from playing weapon cards, the least common of the three card types. Some spells and minion Battlecries and Deathrattles also grant players weapons, as does the rogue hero power Dagger Mastery. As well as equipping weapons, some cards offer weapon synergy, improving or drawing strength from them, or even destroying the enemy's. Pirate cards have a strong weapon theme.

Notes[edit | edit source]

  • Weapons are only active or 'unsheathed' during the possessing hero's turn.[1] If the hero is attacked during their opponent's turn, the weapon will not be used, and will not deal damage or lose Durability.
  • Weapons only lose Durability when used to attack a target. If a hero is attacked during their own turn, such as through Misdirection, the weapon will deal damage but will not lose Durability.
  • Weapons can be destroyed through running out of Durability; being replaced by another weapon; or specific destroy effects like those of Acidic Swamp Ooze and Harrison Jones. Regardless of the means, the effect is the same, and any Deathrattles on the weapon will be triggered.
  • Weapons serve to increase the hero's Attack, but this can be increased further through effects such as Shapeshift and Heroic Strike. The damage dealt by the hero's attacks is equal to their total Attack value, not the attack of any weapon equipped.
  • Weapons are the only enduring source of hero Attack; all other sources are lost at the end of the turn.
  • Since all weapon cards have a dark gray background, the class a weapon belongs to is indicated by the color of the frame surrounding the card text. On golden weapon cards class is indicated by the color of the vertical band running down the centre of the card.

Classes[edit | edit source]

All classes except druids, priests and mages have weapons available to them. Rogues, paladins and warriors are generally considered to have the greatest synergy with weapons, while hunters and shamans have limited weapon choices. Warlocks do not generally have access to weapons, but gain one when replaced by Lord Jaraxxus.

All heroes can equip weapons, and even weaponless classes can obtain them through a combination of mind control effects and return effects. For example, a mage might use Sylvanas Windrunner's Deathrattle to take control of an Arathi Weaponsmith, then use Youthful Brewmaster to return it to their hand, allowing it to be played again, equipping the hero with Battle Axe. Additionally, Blingtron 3000 equips both players with a weapon, even if one of the players is a usually weaponless class.

Despite not having any weapons of their own, priests have substantial access to opponents' weapons through copy effects such as Mind Vision and Thoughtsteal, as well as Mindgames. With access to powerful additional mind control effects such as Mind Control and Cabal Shadow Priest, the priest is a substantially weapon-capable class when playing against a suitable opponent.
